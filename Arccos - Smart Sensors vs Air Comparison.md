## Executive Summary

Arccos offers two main shot-tracking systems: **Smart Sensors** and **Arccos Air**. Smart Sensors are small screw-in devices (about 7.3 g each) that you install on every club (15 standard clubs + 1 putter in a full set) and pair to the Arccos app via your smartphone. They use built-in motion detectors and GPS from your phone (or Arccos Air) to automatically record every swing and putt. Each Smart Sensor is weather-resistant and uses a sealed battery designed to last **at least 2 years** (with a 2-year warranty) without recharging. By contrast, **Arccos Air** is a single pocket-worn AI device (roughly the size of an AirPods case) that contains accelerometers, a gyroscope and GPS so it can detect every shot on its own–_no club sensors and no phone needed while playing_. Arccos Air has an internal rechargeable battery (about **10 hours**, roughly 2 rounds, per charge) and comes with a charging case that holds about 12 rounds' worth of power.

Both systems feed data into the same Arccos app and cloud service, delivering full features like shot-by-shot tracking, distances, strokes-gained analytics, AI caddie strategy and green maps. In short, Smart Sensors give the most precise club-by-club data (since each shot is tagged by a specific sensor on the club), while Arccos Air trades that extra accuracy for the convenience of a single wearable unit and phone-free play. Each requires an active Arccos Game Tracking subscription (first year included with new hardware), and both are compatible with modern iPhones (iOS 16/17+) and many Androids (Android 11+).

## Pros & Cons

**Arccos Smart Sensors (Gen 4)**

- **Pros:** Very precise auto-tagging of clubs–you get exact club distances and strokes-gained stats for each club. No need to recharge (2-year permanent battery) and they are extremely light (≈7.3 g). Quick install (twist into most grips). Proven PGA Tour–used system with mature software. Robust shot detection (rarely misses swings) and full Arccos analytics (AI caddie, green maps, etc.). Two-year replacement guarantee and 5 free sensors per year for extras.
- **Cons:** Requires carrying a smartphone (or Air device) on the course–the phone's GPS and radio must stay on, which can drain battery. Setup (pairing) of up to 16 sensors initially takes time. Hardware on clubs adds a few grams to swing weight (most adapt easily). Very soft putts/taps can occasionally go undetected, though Arccos's software compensates. Must replace sensors after ~2 years (Arccos's renewal program covers this). Moderate upfront cost and annual subscription required (though first year is free).

**Arccos Air (AI Wearable)**

- **Pros:** Completely sensorless and phone-free during play–just drop Air in your pocket or clip it on and go. Surprisingly accurate auto-detection of shots (reviewer found "near-complete shot detection") and full suite of Arccos statistics afterward. Eliminates the need to carry a phone on the course (you can even play music or take calls on your phone while Air records in the background). Lighting-fast setup and easy editing. Existing Arccos users get a free firmware upgrade (Link Pro → Air). Lightweight unit (<25 g), modern design, with charging case for convenience.
- **Cons:** Club identification is done by AI inference rather than a physical tag, so club distances are slightly less foolproof (some post-round editing of club selection may be needed). Higher cost overall (Air + subscription vs. sensors only). Currently, true sensorless mode is only fully supported on iOS; Android users must still carry sensors (Arccos promises Android sensorless "soon"). If the Air's battery dies mid-round (after ~2 rounds), shot tracking will pause until recharged. No hardware on the clubs means you lose automatic backup of exact club IDs–everything depends on the AI accuracy.

## Recommended Scenarios

- **Use Smart Sensors if:** You want the most precise club-by-club data and don't mind installing hardware on each club. For example, a data-driven player with a full set of clubs will benefit from the automatic club distances and minimal post-round edits. Smart Sensors are ideal if you already carry a smartphone (or use the Arccos Watch app) and want a proven system. They are also necessary if you have an Android phone today (since Arccos Air's sensorless mode is pending on Android).
- **Use Arccos Air if:** You hate handling gadgets on your clubs or carrying a phone on-course. Arccos Air is great for casual or traveling golfers, renters, or anyone who wants an "unplugged" round. With Air, you can simply play and sync later; it's perfect if you often forget or dislike managing sensors. Also, if you already have a Link Pro unit, you can upgrade seamlessly to the Air experience. Note: Air works best on iOS at present–Android users may need sensors until full support arrives.
- **Combined use:** Many golfers will use both–Smart Sensors + Arccos Air (or Link Pro)–for maximum flexibility. In this setup, Smart Sensors ensure exact club tagging, while Air lets you ditch the phone on the course. This combo is recommended for competitive players who want both convenience and data depth.
